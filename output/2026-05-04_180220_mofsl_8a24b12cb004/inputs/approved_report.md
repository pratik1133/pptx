# Motilal Oswal Financial Services Initiation Note

## Investment View

BUY with SAARTHI Score of 72/100. Motilal Oswal Financial Services represents a compelling investment opportunity driven by its accelerating transformation from a cyclical broking firm into a high-quality, annuity-driven financial services platform. Annual Recurring Revenue (ARR) now constitutes 65% of total net revenues — with management guiding for further increases — fundamentally re-rating the quality of the earnings base. CMP of INR 822.90 offers a 37% discount to analyst consensus target of INR 1,127, with a probability-weighted target of INR 1,109.

## Why We Like It

- ARR contribution at 65% of total net revenues, reducing cyclical broking volatility
- Q2FY25 profits up 123% YoY with 46% revenue growth and 48% ROE
- Total AUM at INR 1.77 lakh crore (+46% YoY); 91% of AUM outperforming benchmarks over 3 years
- Private Wealth AUM at INR 1.96 lakh crore (+31% YoY); family count up 41% YoY
- Long-term credit rating upgraded to AA+ (Stable) by ICRA
- Reinvestment Quality 13/15 — disciplined capital allocation into fee-based businesses
- 91% of AMC AUM outperforming benchmarks supports premium fee retention

## Industry Context

Indian Financial Services is a strategic shock absorber for the growth story. India is expected to have 16.57 lakh HNWIs by 2027 and become the fourth-largest private wealth market by 2028, with a 58.4% projected rise in UHNWIs. The financial services sector is projected to nearly double profits by FY30, led by NBFCs growing at 16% annually. Mutual fund penetration at just 5–6% reflects substantial latent growth opportunity. Private credit hit a record USD 9.0 billion in H1 2025, up 53% YoY.

## Key Catalysts

- ARR contribution crossing 70% of revenues, triggering valuation re-rating from broking to asset management multiples
- Sustained AMC AUM growth above 25% YoY with net flows exceeding INR 10,000 crore annually
- Private wealth AUM growth above 25% YoY with family count expansion
- Operating leverage improvement with cost-to-income ratio declining
- Regulatory clarity on F&O trading norms

## Key Risks

- SEBI regulatory headwinds on F&O trading norms compressing cyclical broking revenues
- Q2FY26 profit declined 67.6% YoY and revenue 34.9% YoY due to regulatory changes — residual cyclicality
- Competitive intensity from discount brokers and fintech platforms compressing yields
- Forensic / governance: recurring SEBI matters require monitoring (Monitor Closely category)
- Mark-to-market volatility on the proprietary book
- Housing finance asset-quality slippage in stressed credit cycles
